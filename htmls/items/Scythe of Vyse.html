The Scythe of Vyse , also known as Sheepstick , is an Item purchasable at the Main Shop, under Caster . However, it requires items from the Secret Shop to be completed.

Additional information [ edit | edit source ]

  • One Scythe of Vyse grants a total of 200 health , 0.3 health regeneration , 1.43 armor , 10 attack speed , 420 mana , 1.4 (+ 150%) mana regeneration , 2.1875% spell damage and 10 (35 to intelligence heroes) attack damage .

Notes:

  • Applies a Hex on the target, setting its base movement speed to 140 and silencing , muting and disarming it.
  • Because hex changes base move speed and does not actually apply a slow , all speed effects are calculated using the 140 base speed.
  • For example:
    • a hexed unit with no speed bonuses has 140 move speed
    • a hexed unit with +8% from Yasha has 151.2 move speed
    • a hexed unit with +45 from Boots of Speed has 185 move speed
    • a hexed unit with both Boots of Speed and Yasha has 199.8 move speed
  • Hex instantly destroys illusions , but not summoned units .
  • Scythe of Vyse hex transforms the target into a pig.

Tips [ edit | edit source ]

  • Combine it with a blink or invisibility to surprise your target and approchach and hit him while he is hexed. You can also use those items to catch an enemy that is running away.
  • If your enemy has a Linken's Sphere you can buy a Force Staff which is the cheapest item to disable it before applying hex.

Trivia [ edit | edit source ]

  • In the original DotA, this item was known as Guinsoo's Scythe of Vyse , as a tribute to one of the developers of DotA: Allstars , who went on to co-create League of Legends .
  • This item is known by many players (and referred to by several in-game Heroes) as " Sheepstick " due to turning targeted heroes into sheep in DotA. However, in Dota 2, targeted units turn into a pig.
    • Many heroes that have responses for buying Scythe of Vyse also call it " Sheepstick ".
  • The item's name may be a reference to the Sega RPG Skies of Arcadia . The main character, Vyse, has been depicted weilding a scythe in early concept art.
